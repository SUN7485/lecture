Photosynthesis

Photosynthesis is the process by which green plants convert light energy into chemical energy stored in glucose.

1. The Overall Reaction

Plants take in carbon dioxide and water, and using sunlight captured by chlorophyll, produce glucose and oxygen.

6 CO₂ + 6 H₂O + light → C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6 O₂

2. The Two Stages

The light-dependent reactions occur in the thylakoid membranes and produce ATP and NADPH.

The Calvin cycle takes place in the stroma and uses that energy to fix carbon into sugar.

3. Why It Matters

Photosynthesis is the foundation of nearly every food chain on Earth and is responsible for the oxygen we breathe.

Understanding it helps us appreciate agriculture, climate, and the carbon cycle.
